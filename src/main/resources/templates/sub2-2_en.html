We strengthen the structural competitiveness of the used-car export industry and drive key projects that enable stable, sustainable growth for our members.

Key Projects

Export complex development for used cars

We build clustered infrastructure for used-car exports so inspection, storage, maintenance, customs, and shipping are seamlessly connected. This reduces export costs and lead time while expanding globally competitive export hubs based on standardized operations.

Build a one-stop export support system

We connect the entire export process—from registration and deregistration to customs, shipping, and logistics—into a single one-stop system, greatly reducing operational burden for members. Digital automation improves transparency and accuracy while enabling fast, stable export operations.

Joint logistics and cost reduction

We operate a joint logistics system to structurally reduce key costs such as transport, shipping, and storage, easing the burden on individual companies. Collective negotiation and economies of scale provide members with a reasonable cost structure and stable logistics services.

Joint overseas market development and buyer trust management

We analyze overseas market trends through regional committees and establish joint entry strategies for promising markets. Sharing buyer credit information and managing transaction histories helps prevent poor transactions and risks, creating a stable overseas trading environment.

Fair competition and member protection

We coordinate and mediate among members to prevent excessive and irrational price competition in overseas markets. This establishes fair competition, protects member interests, and enhances industry-wide trust.

Eco-friendly exports and sustainable logistics

We establish eco-friendly export standards and logistics guidelines in response to international regulations and global ESG trends, gradually building export structures that minimize environmental impact. This is a key project to secure long-term sustainability for the industry.

Regulatory improvements and policy cooperation

We continuously identify regulatory gaps related to used-car exports and pursue reasonable improvements through coordination with government and relevant institutions. The association actively participates as a channel for industry voices in policy collaboration and regulatory refinement.

Information sharing and digital transformation support

We foster information exchange among members and build shared IT systems and data-driven operations to promote digital transformation across the industry. This enables members to make more structured, data-based export decisions.
